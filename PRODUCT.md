# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Oloráculo is for people reviewing World Cup prediction quality and data readiness: the project owner, maintainers, and technically curious football users who need to understand why a prediction is credible or degraded. They are usually checking model outputs, source freshness, API enrichment status, and whether the current data pipeline is trustworthy enough to use.

## Product Purpose

Oloráculo predicts 2026 FIFA World Cup matches and tournament outcomes while making the model's evidence visible. Success means users can quickly tell which signals are active, which inputs are stale or unavailable, where the model is degraded, and what action is needed to improve prediction quality.

## Brand Personality

Sporty, energetic, and trustworthy. The interface should feel like a football analysis cockpit: alive and match-aware, but still operationally clear. Energy should come from prioritization, state, rhythm, and football context — not from decorative noise.

## Anti-references

Do not make Oloráculo look like a gambling or betting app. It uses probabilities, but the experience must communicate analysis and trust rather than wagering.

Do not make it a generic SaaS dashboard with interchangeable gray cards, context-free metrics, and no football personality.

Do not make it a promotional FIFA-style marketing page with oversized hero treatment, excessive flags, shine, or spectacle. The product must explain data quality and prediction confidence, not sell hype.

## Design Principles

- Make data trust visible: every source status should distinguish configured, fresh, stale, missing, failed, and blocked-by-provider states.
- Explain degradation clearly: users should know when predictions use enriched context and when they fall back to simpler signals.
- Keep probability sober: percentages are analytical evidence, not betting prompts.
- Preserve football energy without decoration: use team, fixture, and competition context deliberately, not as visual clutter.
- Prefer operational actionability: every warning should point to the next useful fix or decision.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Maintain strong contrast, keyboard-accessible navigation and controls, clear loading/error/success states, and readable dense data. Motion should be minimal, state-driven, and respect reduced-motion preferences.
